The Lexicon

A comprehensive record of all documented phonemes and words from the Tongue, maintained by the Custodia Ultimae Vocis since 390 AD. Entries are classified by tier based on frequency of occurrence and confidence of translation.

Tier Classification:

  • Tier One: 15+ occurrences, confirmed translation
  • Tier Two: 5-14 occurrences, probable translation
  • Tier Three: 1-4 occurrences, hypothetical translation

Tier One (Confirmed)

kheth
/kɛθ/ - KETH
Tier One | 17 occurrences

Translation: threshold, boundary, liminal space

First Documented: TM-0033-001 (Year 33 AD)

Context: Appears in death scenarios involving transition or crossing. Often paired with other threshold-related terms.

voram
/voɹam/ - VOR-ahm
Tier One | 23 occurrences

Translation: witness, observer, one who sees

First Documented: TM-0067-003 (Year 67 AD)

Context: Frequently appears in cases with multiple witnesses present at death. May indicate relationship between observer and observed.

ghur
/ɡuɹ/ - GOOR
Tier One | 19 occurrences

Translation: consume, devour, hunger

First Documented: TM-0156-012 (Year 156 AD)

Context: Associated with violent deaths or deaths involving loss/consumption. Church interpretation: spiritual hunger for divine presence.

loresh
/loɹɛʃ/ - LOR-esh
Tier One | 21 occurrences

Translation: memory, past, what was

First Documented: TM-0089-005 (Year 89 AD)

Context: Often appears in elderly subjects or deaths involving remembrance. Temporal component unclear.

Tier Two (Probable)

marak
/maɹak/ - MAH-rahk
Tier Two | 12 occurrences

Translation: becoming, forming, emergence (hypothetical)

First Documented: TM-0234-008 (Year 234 AD)

Context: Appears in births, transformations, or sudden onset deaths. Translation disputed among translators.

nem
/nɛm/ - NEM
Tier Two | 9 occurrences

Translation: breath, air, life-force

First Documented: TM-0445-015 (Year 445 AD)

Context: Associated with respiratory deaths. Church interpretation: the soul departing as breath.

Tier Three (Hypothetical)

thorum
/θoɹum/ - THOR-um
Tier Three | 3 occurrences

Translation: whole, complete, one (speculative)

First Documented: TM-1823-042 (Year 1823 AD)

Context: Extremely rare. All three occurrences in last 200 years. Pattern unclear. Further analysis required.
