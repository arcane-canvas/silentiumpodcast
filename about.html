About SILENTIUM

SILENTIUM is a solo-narrator horror fiction podcast following Vatican linguist Father Tommaso Lanza as he investigates deaths for the Custodia Ultimae Vocis—a secret Church institution studying an impossible language that manifests at the moment of death.

Each episode documents a new case as Father Tommaso attempts to translate the Tongue, an ancient language that has been appearing in dying people's mouths for over 2,000 years. What begins as academic documentation slowly reveals a terrifying pattern: all manifestations are fragments of the same sentence, and it's nearly complete.

Format

SILENTIUM uses a first-person investigation format. Father Tommaso reconstructs cases from evidence, recordings, and interviews, documenting his findings in personal research logs. Episodes are 28-35 minutes, released [weekly/biweekly].

The show features:

  • Solo voice performance with processed multi-layered audio for the Tongue
  • Binaural sound design for immersive atmosphere
  • Hidden spectral images in audio (view in Audacity for easter eggs)
  • Constructed language with consistent rules for community decoding
  • Anthology structure with overarching mythology

Hidden Content Guide

SILENTIUM contains hidden visual content embedded in the audio using spectrogram steganography. To view:

  1. Download the episode audio (high quality recommended)
  2. Open in Audacity (free software)
  3. Switch to Spectrogram view (click track dropdown > Spectrogram)
  4. Look for images in the 18-22 kHz frequency range

Hidden content includes throat sigils, case metadata, lexicon entries, and pattern clues. The community maintains a catalog of all discovered content.
